Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and the hottest in the Solar System due to a runaway greenhouse effect. A dense CO2 atmosphere and sulfuric acid clouds produce surface temperatures of 465 C and pressures 92 times Earth. Venus rotates retrograde, its day being longer than its year.

Active missions

Mariner 2 (NASA)
Flyby 1962
First successful planetary flyby. Confirmed extreme surface temperatures.
Venera 7 (USSR)
Landed 1970-12-15
First spacecraft to soft-land on another planet. Transmitted data for 23 minutes from the surface.
Venera 13 (USSR)
Landed 1982-03-01
First color photographs from Venus surface. Survived 127 minutes and analyzed soil composition.
Magellan (NASA)
Orbit 1990-1994
Radar-mapped 98% of the surface at 100m resolution through the cloud layer.
Venus Express (ESA)
Orbit 2006-2014
Studied atmosphere, plasma environment, and surface heat emission.
Akatsuki (JAXA)
Orbit since 2015
Currently active Japanese climate orbiter studying cloud dynamics and atmospheric circulation in multi-wavelength imagery.

Notable features

Maxwell Montes
Highest mountain on Venus (~11 km). Possibly coated with metallic frost (lead or bismuth sulfide).
Aphrodite Terra
Continent-sized highland region near the equator.
Ishtar Terra
Northern highland region containing Maxwell Montes.
Greenhouse atmosphere
Thick CO2 blanket traps heat; supercritical CO2 at the surface behaves more like a fluid than a gas.
